CSR

Farmers Education

Farmers education is a post season industry focused initiative set up to educate farmers on planting, nurturing and post harvest measuring methods to help improve quality of nuts, quantity of yield and to decrease the amount of wasted crop. This builds a relationship between the company and grower, helps engage with issues farmers face in the industry and educates them on what happens to their crops after sale. Farmers day is not just focused on macadamia but also educates famers on integrated farming to support the social wellbeing of the growers and their family, this can include agricultural methods of which crops grow well together e.g. spring/salad onion & green peppers. The farmers education scheme has seen an increase of participants from 2015 only catering to mere 100 growers to attracting 200+ farmers in 2016.

Daycare Centre/ Library for Staff’s Children

The Village Nut received investment after the 2016 season from a partner to help fund a community project after a survey a Staff daycare Centre and library for children from the ages 0-3 and a library for children aged 3-14 was decided. This was chosen due to many factors, children are left under the supervision of distant family members when parents are at work, making it difficult for parents to work when arrangements are not made. There are also numerous staff members who are or due to be nursing mothers by the beginning of the new season, this gives the opportunity for feeding regimes to remain undisrupted as this is critical to their health. The daycare Centre also encourages education for the children allowing them access to a professional teacher.

Supporting local schools

The Company Directors have been members of the board of management in both local primary and secondary school’s for generations, giving support and donations to keep maintenance of the education system in the area. This has been through assistance of construction projects, purchasing education materials, funding events and prize giving as incentives for top performing students. This is essential to promote and encourage education in the local area, due to the fact that a lot of the staff either schooled in these schools or have children or family members that attend these schools.
